01 · The Market

Why Bend is a great first market

You're not picking Bend by accident — its economy is almost perfectly shaped for a home-services marketplace.

  • Fast-growing & affluent. One of the fastest-growing metros in the U.S., with high median home values and lots of in-migration from California and Portland — homeowners with money to spend on services.
  • Huge vacation-rental turnover economy. Central Oregon runs on tourism. Short-term rentals need frequent cleaning, handyman, and landscaping turnovers — recurring, high-frequency demand that spins your flywheel fast.
  • Booming new construction & remodels. NW Crossing, NE Bend, Petrosa — constant renovation, move-ins, and cleanouts (great for junk removal, painting, cleaning).
  • Strong seasons = year-round demand. Snowy winters (heating, snow removal, gutters) and hot, dry, wildfire-prone summers (AC, irrigation, defensible-space landscaping, pressure washing).
  • Tight-knit online community. Bend has unusually active Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and an engaged r/Bend — meaning free, high-trust demand channels where "anyone know a good cleaner?" posts happen daily.

Your unfair advantage

You already understand the junk-removal world through Northwest Junk Pros. That means you know how to find, vet, and talk to local service operators — the single hardest part of building a marketplace. Use it.

02 · The Wedge

Which trade to launch with

Don't launch all 10 categories. Win one trade completely, then expand. Pick a trade with high frequency so transactions (and reviews) pile up quickly.

Recommended: start with House Cleaning 🧹

It's the fastest flywheel in Bend — vacation-rental turnovers happen almost daily and residential cleaning repeats weekly/biweekly. Frequent jobs = fast reviews = quick proof the model works. There are also plenty of cleaners to recruit.

Then fast-follow with two more in month 2–3:

  • Junk Removal — your wheelhouse. Higher ticket, strong demand from moves & remodels, and you can personally vouch for quality.
  • Landscaping / Lawn & Yard — high volume in Bend spring–fall, plus snow removal in winter.

Prefer to lead with junk removal instead? Totally valid given your experience — it lowers your execution risk on quality and recruiting. The only tradeoff: junk removal is lower-frequency (a household needs it ~once or twice a year), so your flywheel spins slower. If you go this route, lean harder on B2B partners (realtors, property managers, contractors) who generate repeat volume.

03 · The Strategy

The core strategy: supply first

Every marketplace faces the chicken-and-egg problem: homeowners won't come without pros, pros won't stay without homeowners. Here's how you break it without spending millions like Thumbtack did.

  1. Recruit a small founding roster first (10–20 cleaners). So when the first homeowner shows up, there's someone ready to serve them.
  2. Be the matchmaker manually at the start ("concierge MVP"). You don't need fancy software — when a request comes in, you personally text it to 2–3 matching pros. This is how Airbnb, DoorDash, and Thumbtack all started.
  3. Turn on demand in a tight radius (just Bend, not all of Oregon). Concentrated demand keeps your founding pros busy and happy.
  4. Harvest reviews relentlessly. Every completed job → ask for a review. Reviews are the fuel that makes the site credible to the next homeowner.
  5. Only then scale supply & add trades. Expand once pros are getting real jobs and asking you for more.
04 · The Plan

The 90-day launch plan

Three phases. Each builds on the last. Don't skip ahead.

Week 1

Phase 0 — Foundation

Goal: be ready to take a lead by the end of the week
  • Add "Serving Bend & Central Oregon" to the Housio site; set the launch trade to House Cleaning.
  • Grab a domain, a free Google Voice business number, and a Google Business Profile (huge for local search — free).
  • Set up a dead-simple back office: an Airtable or Google Sheet for pros + leads, and your phone for matching. (No need to build the real backend yet.)
  • Lock in your Founding Pro offer (see scripts below): first 90 days free, founding badge, locked-in founder rate.
Weeks 2–4

Phase 1 — Recruit your founding pros

Goal: 15–20 cleaners signed up and ready
  • Build a list of 50+ Bend cleaners (sources listed in the scripts section).
  • Work the outreach scripts: text and call first (best response from tradespeople), email/DM as backup.
  • Onboard 15–20. Explain the fair model, collect their service area + best contact, set expectations.
  • Prioritize pros already paying for Thumbtack/Angi — they feel the pain most and convert fastest.
Weeks 4–8

Phase 2 — Turn on demand, deliver first jobs

Goal: 25–50 completed jobs with reviews
  • Publish local SEO pages: "House Cleaning in Bend, OR," "Vacation Rental Cleaning Bend," etc.
  • Run a small Google Ads / Local Services Ads budget ($300–600/mo) for "house cleaning Bend."
  • Post and answer in Bend Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and r/Bend (free demand — see channel list).
  • Land 2–3 B2B partners: a property manager, a realtor, an Airbnb host. These generate repeat volume.
  • Match leads to pros manually. After every job: ask the homeowner for a review.
Weeks 8–13

Phase 3 — Prove the loop & expand

Goal: a repeatable weekly rhythm + first paying pros
  • Measure your unit economics: cost per homeowner lead, lead→job conversion, pro retention.
  • Add Junk Removal and Landscaping as trades #2 and #3.
  • Convert founding pros to paid: a flat monthly subscription (~$99–$149/mo) or pay-only-when-a-customer-replies.
  • When the loop is humming, that's your signal to build the real backend (Supabase + Stripe) and, later, the apps.

08 · Objections

Handling the 5 objections you'll hear

"I already use Thumbtack / Angi."
"Perfect — then you know exactly the problem I'm solving. Keep using them. Housio is free for your first 90 days, so there's zero risk to add it as another source of jobs. If my leads are better, you'll naturally do more with me. If not, you've lost nothing."
"I'm too busy / already booked out."
"Love to hear it — that tells me you do great work, which is exactly who I want. No obligation to take every lead. When you have a gap, or want a higher-value job, Housio's there. And founding spots won't be free forever."
"What's the catch? Why is it free?"
"Honest answer: I'm launching in Bend and I need great pros to make it work. Free for founding pros is how I earn your trust. Down the road I make money on a small flat fee — but only from pros who are getting real jobs. If you're not winning work, I'm not charging you."
"How do you even have customers yet?"
"Fair question — I'm running ads and working Bend's local groups and property managers right now. I'm lining up pros first so that when a homeowner asks, I've got someone great ready. You'd be in from day one, which is why founding pros get the best deal."
"I tried something like this before and it flopped."
"I get it — most of these treat pros like ATMs. That's the whole reason Housio caps leads at 3–4 pros, refunds junk leads, and only charges when a customer replies. Try it free for 90 days and judge it on the jobs, not the promise."
09 · The Apps

The path to iOS & Android apps

You'll want native apps — but the smart move is to earn them, not start with them. Here's the sequence so you don't burn money building an app nobody uses yet.

  1. Now → web works on phones already. Your Housio site already works in any phone browser. That's enough to launch and validate in Bend.
  2. Next → make it a PWA (installable web app). A small upgrade lets homeowners and pros "Add to Home Screen" — it looks and feels like an app, works offline-ish, sends notifications, and costs almost nothing. Great middle step.
  3. Then → real native apps once the loop is proven. When you have steady jobs flowing, we build true iOS + Android apps from one codebase using React Native (Expo) — so you're not paying to build two separate apps. This is also when the Supabase + Stripe backend becomes essential (the apps need a real server to talk to).

When you're ready, I can help with all of it — turning Housio into a PWA first, then scaffolding the React Native app, wiring up Supabase auth, Stripe payments, and push notifications. Just say the word and we'll start with the PWA.
